Correlations Observed Between Health Risks, Age and Income

This project is to create a D3.js scatter plot using data from the 2014 American Community Survey (ACS) 1-year estimates to observe any trends between variables such as age, income and potential health risks. Please select a label on either axis to explore different plots.

A positive correlation can be discerned between health related risk factors and percentage of population in poverty while there is a clear negative correlation between health related risk factors and median household income. There seems to be a less obvious relationship between health related risk factors and age, with a slight positive correlation between percent of population that smoke and age, a slight negative correlation between percent of population that lack healthcare and age, and no apparently relationship between obesity and age.
